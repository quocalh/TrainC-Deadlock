## Problem Statement
  Mr. Minh opened a small convenience store near a school in Go Vap District—A convenient store where students visit every day for instant noodles, bottled drinks, and all sorts of small items. Customers come and go, but behind the counter is a “mess” that he struggles to manage.
  The stock in the storage area is so large that he can’t keep track of everything. Sometimes he overorders, filling the shelves with excess goods. Other times, he runs out of popular items without realizing it, and only notices when customers ask—leaving him with nothing to offer, leaving him with no option but to decline.
  At the moment, everything is recorded in a notebook. Every purchase or sale is manually added or subtracted. However, this method is increasingly showing its limitations: the numbers often don’t match reality, he can’t tell which items are running low to restock in time, and by the end of the month, he’s almost “stuck” when trying to calculate profits because the data isn’t accurate or complete.
## Problem constraints
- The system can store up to 10,000 products and 100,000 inventory transactions (stock in/out).
- Each product must be uniquely identified by an ID product, with no duplicates allowed.
- Data must be saved to a file after every operation, ensuring no data loss if the program is closed or there is a sudden power outage.
